Homeowner Accountability Console

A private workspace to gather key mortgage, land, and tax records and see what may need a closer look.

Start by gathering what you have. You do not need every document today. Each section explains what belongs there and how to find it.

1. Case basics

These fields stay at the top of every printout so anyone you share with can see the core details.

If you are not sure of the county, look at your tax bill or search your address online. County information helps you find land and tax records.

2. What this tool does

  • • Keeps statements, escrow analyses, land records, tax records, and letters in one place.
  • • Tracks what you already have and what is still missing.
  • • Gives prompts for possible servicing problems to note in your own words.
  • • Helps you create a simple summary you can print or read over the phone with a helper.

3. Document buckets

For each bucket, check off what you have and use the notes area to list file names, dates, or where you saved them (folder name, phone, USB drive).

Monthly statements

Goal: At least the last 24 months, plus any “catch‑up” or default statements.

Escrow analyses

Goal: Every escrow analysis for years when your payment changed.

Land records (deed & liens)

Goal: Deed, mortgage / deed of trust, and any assignments or releases recorded at the county.

Tax & PVA records

Goal: Most recent tax bill(s), proof of tax payment, and PVA/assessor screen showing owner & mailing address.

Notices, letters & court papers

Goal: Keep every notice of default, acceleration, loss‑mitigation, modification offer, and any court filings.

Call logs & screenshots

Goal: A simple log of calls, plus screenshots of online account history where possible.

4. Where to find your own records

Use these prompts to search your county and online tools. Write down the web addresses and any login details you use so you can return later.

This tool does not visit those sites for you. Instead, it helps you keep track of what each site says about your home so you can spot differences.

5. Servicing issues & patterns

Use your own words. You do not have to sound like a lawyer. Focus on dates, amounts, and what you were told.

6. Summary & next steps

Use the buttons below to quickly mark where you are. This does not send data anywhere. It is only for you to see your own progress.

Status snapshot (for you)

  • Goal Have 24 months of statements, escrow analyses for each change, and recent tax bills in one place.
  • Check If any payment changes do not match escrow letters or tax/insurance changes, note them in the “Issues” tab.
  • Urgent If you see fees or default notices that do not match your records, consider speaking with a housing counselor or legal aid office using this worksheet as your story.

Your own next steps

This console does not give legal advice or make decisions for you. It helps you see your own records more clearly so you can decide what to do next or speak with someone who can help.
